Marino - the scietist who cleaned a lake for 4 months.

Inspiring people are all around us. Artists, innovators, scientists who give us hope and motivation.

However, the sad truth is that the media does not talk about them. The man in this article is like a

super hero.

Marino Morikawa is Peruvian-Japanese scientist who have cleaned a lake all by himself. When

he was a kid, he used to play near Cascajo Wetlands and fish in it. He then went to study in

Japan where he god a PhD in science. He was working at Tsukuba University when he learned

from his father that his childhood’s lake is contaminated because of the human’s greed. Birds and

fish had long abandoned the area and local authorities were losing hope of recovering the lake.

However, Marino did not give up. He knew that when the government cannot solve the problem,

he had to do something about it. He talked with the authorities of the area and promised that he

would save the lake in one year with no cost from their past.

Morikawa identified two key factors which had resulted in the extreme state of the wetland: illegal

landfills and the breeding of pigs, cattle and sheep. The former mayor of the region had also built

a drainage canal that bordered the wetland and which had leaked into it.

He took a break from school, went to a bank, got a loan and dedicated his time to fix the lake. He

came up with a unique solution. His solution attracts the contaminated particles and floats them to

the top. This is so environmentally friendly that you can eat it, because is a 100% organic.

One day he went to Cascajo Wetlands and dropped tons of his solution using two nanotechnology

methods to remove the pollution and noxious microorganisms: the micro-nano bubbling system

and a biofilter. Everything, that he used, was made locally and was organic, because as he

says it is not needed to use expensive resource in poor area while you can make it home. After

developing this nanotechnology for 6 months, he cleaned the lake after 4 months. Because of

this, more than 40 migratory birds’ species returned to the lake and 10 fish species populated the

waters. The locals were inspired by Marino’s action and hard work and soon came to help him.

When Marino was asked by a youtuber, who made a video for him, why nobody do that, he said

that this is a very expensive and very hard thing, but also said that it is not impossible. That is

why Marino is thinking big. He wants to clean Lake Titicaca, Peru’s biggest lake. He now has an

entire time, PhDs in science, millions of dollars in funding, patented technology and a noble goal

of making the world cleaner for everybody.
